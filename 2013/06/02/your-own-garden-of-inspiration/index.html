Your own garden of inspiration

June 2, 2013Confidence, Courage, Inspiration, Motivation, Self-beliefConfidence, Courage, Inspiration, Motivation, Self-beliefjfdikarla

A visit to Monet’s Garden exhibition at the NGV today made me think about sources of inspiration and how it can be difficult to be different. Monet broke free from the style of artists before him, although this practically ousted him from the art community at the time. While other artists were mimicking techniques from old masters, Monet took his canvas outside and painted what he saw. Surrounding himself with the beauty of nature, Monet was continually inspired by his garden. He knew what stirred his creativity and chose to be as close to it as possible. I left the exhibition feeling deep respect for a man who was so strong in his pursuits, so fuelled by his inspiration, that he revolutionised the entire art world. To live a life of true, creative expression isn’t easy. To allow inspiration in and creativity out takes courage, confidence and a willingness to persevere when others don’t always believe in you. If we surround ourselves with whatever rouses our passion—have our own ‘gardens of inspiration’—and truly believe in ourselves, there’s no limit to how far we can go.
