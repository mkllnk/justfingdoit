Balance or contradiction?

July 29, 2013Balance, Change, Choices, OpportunityBalance, Change, Choices, Opportunityjfdikarla

If there’s one thing I’ve learned in life, it’s that ‘balance’ is very important. But of course knowing I need to have balance is one thing, finding it is another.

When I look at my diary, in one week it says ‘yoga class’, ‘drinks with…’, ‘naturopath appointment’, ‘brunch/dinner with…’, ‘raw food alkalising workshop’, ‘birthday party’, ‘meditation’.

And there’s so much more that I don’t write down—my thought processes and more minor activities seem even more polar…

making green smoothies for breakfast, drinking wine at night
only buying organic dairy products, getting a latte at a café
aiming for 10,000 steps a day, slobbing on the couch
taking deep breaths, rushing around like a crazy woman
living in the moment, worrying about the future
eschewing materialism, buying a gorgeous new ring
standing up for my beliefs, running away from a spider

How can all these activities and emotions exist in the same space—i.e. me? Is my life a series of contradictions? When I put this question to a friend the other night, she wisely replied “It’s all balance”.

I smiled when she said this as I’ve been trying to work out this illusive ‘balance’ for some time. Why is balance so tricky? We all want so much from life and a lot of our desires seem contradictory. Sometimes it appears easier to go to the extreme and choose certain activities at the exclusion of others, just to try and keep it simple. Oh and how I’ve been so out of balance in the past…

working 50-hour weeks, leaving no time for a personal life
being so into a boy that I drifted away from my friends
being so into my friends that I didn’t have time for a boy
buying my dream car and having no money left each month
becoming so obsessed with my health that I became a social bore
catching up with all my friends, but feeling drained by the end of the week
loving someone so much I sacrificed too much of myself
focusing on myself to such an extent that I shut everyone else out

But surely there’s a way to enjoy more of life’s opportunities without ending up exhausted, lonely or broke?

As with most major life conundrums, there’s no easy answer (you can stop reading now if you think I found it). While I don’t have a magic formula, I’ve found I enjoy a better balance by listening really well to my body’s needs.

If you can interpret how you’re feeling inside, you can make a change before you get totally out of whack. And that means pausing long enough to listen. It takes practice to hear your inner voice, but once you’ve tuned in, it will guide you in your actions.

And, whatever it is, enjoy it. Aim for zero guilt. Why bother having a glass of wine if you’re going to feel bad about it. It’s counter-productive. Yeah it might not be the healthiest thing you can do for your body, but to have negative thoughts about it only compounds the unideal physical effects.

I also try not to do things I feel I just should do, as I’ve found this generally leads to resentment of some sort. The amount of times I’ve felt I should go to an event, when I’ve known I really don’t have the energy for it. Sometimes I do go and end up exhausted…sometimes I don’t go and feel guilty. But what I really try to practice is listening to my needs, making a decision, and then letting go. I either do it whole-heartedly and enjoy every moment, or walk away and move onto the next thing.

When it comes to the real contradictions of life, I ask myself the question Am I ok with that? …and answer honestly. Really, the things I’m contemplating aren’t ‘right’, ‘wrong’, ‘good’, or ‘bad’. They are all just options. My choices are purely a reflection of where I’m at right now—part of the balance I’m shaping in my own life.

And, of course, just when I think I have found the right balance, the parameters shift. I go through a transition. The situation changes. And then it’s a process of relearning what I need. I just keep listening, keep experimenting and my version of balance evolves.

So right now I may be a wine-drinking, green-smoothie-making, caffeine-addicted-yogi, but that feels just fine…for the moment. I know that my balance will need to adjust in time, so I’ll keep welcoming new experiences and checking in with myself along the way, so I can make a change when I need to.
