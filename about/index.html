What is Just F***ing Do It?

Just F***ing Do It is my motto, or mantra, of sorts. What I live by. It summarises my attitude, my life strategy. The way I make decisions.

It began for me a number of years ago when I made some major life changes, put myself out of my comfort zone and confronted my fears.

Just F***ing Do It is a kick-start, a motivation. An inspiration. It helps me listen to my heart and have the courage to follow it. It tells me to just get on with it, when I’m procrastinating; or to make a change when I need to.

Even better, JFDI helps me seize the moment and grasp the power of now. To not waste time feeling unhappy and to understand that what I do today, or purely in this moment, really matters, even though I may have longer term goals as well.

JFDI goes pretty deep for me. Embracing this philosophy has meant I’ve faced up to some tough truths. I was ready for some of them, but not others. About a year before articulating this JFDI philosophy, I’d broken into a new state of realisation about my happiness. It happened in a horribly confronting and shattering way — in that I realised I was desperately unhappy. But that was a huge turning point for me and because I made some changes, my whole life took a different direction. My life journey opened up into a new chapter and this led me on to enjoy a happier way of life. All the way through the transition I was telling myself to Just F***ing Do It.

Another thing to mention is that JDFI isn’t always about doing things for yourself. Sometimes it’s all about someone else. I think motivating yourself for the benefit of others is as good a reason as any.

Why a Just F***ing Do It blog?

As well as a personal motto, JFDI became a project. I started to write about my experiences. Which in doing so, helped me understand them and gain perspective. It was so powerful that I decided to work towards writing a book, to share my JFDI journey. My story isn’t ‘special’ as such, it’s the tale of a ‘normal’ girl in the first world, making choices about her life. But I think that’s what makes it even more important to share. So many of us want to make a change in life, but feel ‘stuck’.

The book is still a work in progress, as I continue to learn and grow and of course it’s difficult to find an ending when you still have so much to learn. So I decided to launch this blog, to at least share some of the smaller fragments of my experiences, and to invite others to share their own.

JFDI has brought so much to my life; opportunities, new experiences, amazing people and ideas. It has allowed me to live life to the full, every day, the way I want… well, most of the time. After times of despair that transformed into triumph I realised getting on with stuff, pushing forward assertively and positively has got me pretty far.
